The Space of Weight k Modular forms \(M_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\) and Cusp Forms \(S_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\)

From the definition of a modular form, it is easy to see that if \(f\) and \(g\) are each modular forms of the same fixed weight k, then \(cf+g\) is also a modular form of weight k for any scalar \(c\). Thus, we deduce that the space of weight k modular forms and weight k cusp forms each form a vector space. We denote the spaces respectively by \(M_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\) and \(S_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\).

The valence and dimension formulas

Given the fundamental domain \(\mathcal{F}\) for \(SL_2(\mathbb{Z})\), we can do a line integral around the boundary of \(\mathcal{F}\) and get that for any nonzero weight k modular form \(f\), we get \[\nu_{i\infty}(f) + \frac{1}{2}\nu_i(f) + \frac{1}{3}\nu_{e^{4\pi i/3}}(f) + \sum_{z \in \mathcal{F} \neq i, e^{2\pi i/3}}'\nu_z(f) = \frac{k}{12}\] where \(\nu_{\tau}(f)\) is the order of the zero of \(f\) at \(\tau\) (which equals 0 if \(f\) is not 0 there), and the ' on the summation means we are only including half the boundary of \(\mathcal{F}\) in the summation.
From this (and a small fact about Ramanujan's weight 12 cusp form \(\Delta\)) we can deduce the exact dimension of the space of weight k modular forms and cusp forms for each k. \[\dim M_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) = \left\{\begin{matrix} \left \lfloor \frac{k}{12} \right \rfloor \textup{ if } k \equiv 2 (\textup{ mod } 12) \\ \left \lfloor \frac{k}{12} +1 \right \rfloor \textup{ if } k \not\equiv 2 (\textup{ mod } 12) \end{matrix}\right.\] The dimension of \(S_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\) is exactly 1 less than \(M_k(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))\), but no less than \(0\).
